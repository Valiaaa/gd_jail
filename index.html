Graphic Design *Jail *
Have you ever heard of “illegal design” before and wondered what it means? Are you curious about the “red flags” of graphic design? Do you wanna know what exactly graphic designers are adjusting and deciding everyday? If you answered yes to any of these questions, then this website is for you!
“Graphic Design Jail” is a series of projects that aim to explore and challenge the current do’s-and-dont’s of graphic design. Below are several design decisions, each presented with options that are theoretically right or wrong. From this website you will hopefully learn how the classic design systems work and eventually make your own decisions.


  1. Stretched Font
  2. Skewed Font
  3. Unhanged Quote
  4. Hyphen & Dash
  5. Poor Font Choice
  6. Bad Text Width
  7. All Caps Text
  8. “Display” Text
  9. Paragraph Separation
  10. Tracking & Leading

  11. Final Challenge

Stretched Font




The typeface should remain how it is designed. If you stretch it, the proportion of its glyph will be distorted.
When you want a condensed / expanded text in your design, just search for a matching variable type.
Width Scale:
30

Skewed Font




The typeface should remain how it is designed. If you skew it, the proportion of its glyph will be distorted.
When you want a italic text in your design, just click the italic option or find a better match.

"This is supposed to be a
hanging smart quote."




When you start a paragraph with a quote, make sure to hang the quotation mark toward the edge so the body text is better aligned.
Also, you need to use the curved (smart) quotation marks instead of the straight ones, which function as units of measurement.
